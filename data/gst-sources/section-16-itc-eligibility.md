# Section 16, CGST Act 2017 — Eligibility and Conditions for taking ITC

> Source: The Central Goods and Services Tax Act, 2017 (public statute).

## Section 16(1)
Every registered person shall, subject to such conditions and restrictions as may
be prescribed, be entitled to take credit of input tax charged on any supply of
goods or services or both to him which are **used or intended to be used in the
course or furtherance of his business**.

## Section 16(2) — Conditions for claiming ITC
No registered person shall be entitled to the credit of any input tax unless:
- (a) he is in possession of a **tax invoice** or debit note issued by a
  registered supplier;
- (aa) the details of the invoice have been **furnished by the supplier** in his
  outward supplies (GSTR-1) and such details have been **communicated to the
  recipient** (this is the legal basis for GSTR-2A / GSTR-2B reconciliation);
- (b) he has **received the goods or services**;
- (c) the **tax charged has actually been paid** to the Government by the
  supplier;
- (d) he has **furnished the return** under section 39.

Where ITC has been availed but the recipient fails to pay the supplier the value
of supply along with tax within **180 days** from the date of invoice, the amount
shall be added to output tax liability with interest (second and third provisos).

## Section 16(4) — Time limit
A registered person shall not be entitled to take ITC in respect of any invoice or
debit note after the **30th day of November** following the end of the financial
year to which such invoice/debit note pertains, or furnishing of the relevant
annual return, whichever is earlier.

## Practical reconciliation rule (Section 16(2)(aa) + Rule 36(4))
ITC can be claimed only to the extent the supplier has reported the invoice and it
appears in the recipient's GSTR-2A / GSTR-2B. Purchases present in the books but
**not reflected in GSTR-2A are "ITC at risk"** — the credit may be denied until
the supplier files. This is the core of TaxMind's reconciliation engine.
